01
Strategic Priority One
Grow Through Evangelisation
✝ Goal · Evangelise

A third of our parish has arrived within the last decade, many coming from other parishes rather than as new Catholics. This signals healthy movement but limited genuine outreach. Only 30% of respondents invited someone to Mass last month — a gap between our personal faith and our public witness. Growing the parish requires intentional, relationship-driven invitation, better newcomer welcome pathways, and embedding a culture of everyday evangelisation across all age groups and cultures.

💡
Key insight: 70% of respondents did NOT invite anyone to Mass last month. Yet 86% attended Mass themselves. The faith is present — the invitation habit is not yet formed.

02
Strategic Priority Two
Deepen Faith Formation
🙏 Goal · Prayer & Holiness

The spiritual depth of our parish is genuinely encouraging — 52% pray daily or more, and the vast majority affirm that faith shapes their lives. However, participation in formal faith formation programmes such as Alpha and OCIA remains low at only 8%, and 52% reported no parish activity beyond attending Mass. There is a significant opportunity to channel existing personal devotion into structured discipleship pathways that deepen faith beyond Sunday and build a culture of lifelong formation.

💡
Key insight: 52% pray daily or more — yet only 8% participate in faith formation programmes. Personal devotion is strong; structured formation is the missing bridge.

03
Strategic Priority Three
Develop Future Parish Leaders
🤝 Goal · Service

Over half of respondents reported zero volunteering last week, and only 13% serve at Mass in roles such as choir or reader. The parish profile skews heavily older — 41% of respondents are over 70 — raising critical questions about leadership succession and the sustainability of current ministries. Identifying, forming, and empowering the next generation of parish leaders is not optional; it is urgent. We must create clear pathways from active parishioner to servant leader, particularly for those aged 31–50 who are most likely to carry the parish forward.

💡
Key insight: 41% of respondents are aged 71+. Only 12% are aged 16–40. Without intentional leadership development now, critical ministries face succession risk within five years.

Strategic Priority Four
Invest in Families and Young People
💬 Goal · Communication & Engagement

Young people aged 16–30 make up only 7% of survey respondents — a stark underrepresentation that reflects a broader challenge facing the Church. Families with school-aged children are present and engaged, yet the parish-school-family partnership needs intentional strengthening. Faith formation for children and young adults must be vibrant, relational, and relevant if we are to retain the next generation. The survey reveals that families are in our pews — the question is whether we are meeting them where they are.

💡
Key insight: Only 7% of respondents are aged 16–30. This is the generation the Church must reach intentionally — through schools, families, and youth-specific programmes that meet them on their terms.

05
Strategic Priority Five
Build Community and Belonging
💬 Goal · Communication & Engagement

The social connection scores are encouraging — over 50% of parishioners report having a good friend at OLOTP and knowing people they can chat with after Mass. However, spiritual connection is weaker: only 19% report that someone asked about their spiritual wellbeing this month, and only 22% asked about someone else's. Our parish is ethnically diverse — NZ European, Filipino, Indian, and many others — and this richness is a gift that requires intentional cultivation to become a genuine source of belonging for all.

💡
Key insight: Social connection is moderate but spiritual connection is weak — only 19% had someone ask about their spiritual wellbeing this month. Parish community must go deeper than friendship into genuine pastoral care.

06
Strategic Priority Six · Additional
Strengthen Stewardship of Time, Talent and Treasure
🤝 Goal · Service

Stewardship is the practical expression of discipleship. The survey reveals that 24% of respondents gave nothing financially last week, while 53% volunteered no time. At the same time, the parish serves a community with moderate to strong household incomes — 57% earn above $40,000 — and a highly educated membership, with 55% holding tertiary qualifications. There is real capacity here that, when connected to a clear vision and personal conviction, can be mobilised to sustain and grow parish life for generations to come.

💡
Key insight: 24% gave nothing last week and 53% volunteered no time. Yet 57% have household incomes above $40k and 55% hold tertiary qualifications. The capacity for greater stewardship is present — the invitation and vision must match it.
